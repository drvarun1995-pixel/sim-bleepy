## üìê **Technical Specifications**

### **Recommended Settings:**
- **Resolution:** 1080p (1920x1080) minimum, 4K if available
- **Frame Rate:** 30fps (standard) or 60fps (smoother)
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 (landscape) - standard for most platforms
- **Format:** MP4 (H.264 codec)
- **Audio:** Clear, no background noise, voice clearly audible

### **Camera Setup:**
- **Position:** Eye-level or slightly above
- **Distance:** Mid-shot (chest up) or close-up (head and shoulders)
- **Focus:** Face sharp and in focus
- **Stability:** Use tripod or stable surface (no shaky cam)

### **Lighting:**
- **Primary:** Face well-lit (natural window light or soft key light)
- **Avoid:** Harsh shadows, backlighting, overexposure
- **Test:** Record 10-second test clip to check lighting before full recording

### **Audio:**
- **Microphone:** Built-in camera mic OR external mic (preferred)
- **Environment:** Quiet room, minimal echo
- **Level:** Voice clear and audible, not too quiet or loud
- **Test:** Record and play back to ensure clarity

## üé¨ **Filming Tips**

### **Before Recording:**
1. **Practice delivery** 3-5 times out loud
2. **Set up camera** at eye level, test framing
3. **Check lighting** - face well-lit, no shadows
4. **Test audio** - record 10-second sample
5. **Clear background** - professional or neutral
6. **Remove distractions** - phone on silent, door closed
7. **Have water** nearby (stay hydrated)

### **During Recording:**
1. **Look at camera lens** (not screen), imagine talking to a friend
2. **Speak naturally** - not robotic or rushed
3. **Use pauses** - brief pauses for emphasis (but stay within 60s)
4. **Show enthusiasm** - smile naturally, show passion
5. **If you make a mistake** - pause, take breath, restart section
6. **Film multiple takes** - aim for 3-5 good takes, pick best

### **After Recording:**
1. **Review all takes** immediately
2. **Identify best take** or best segments
3. **Edit together** best parts (cut out mistakes)
4. **Add text/graphics** if needed
5. **Check timing** - ensure exactly 60 seconds
6. **Export in high quality** - maintain resolution

## üì± **Simple Recording Setup (Minimal Equipment)**

### **If using smartphone:**
1. **Use back camera** (higher quality than front)
2. **Horizontal orientation** (landscape mode)
3. **Use tripod or stable surface** (avoid hand-held)
4. **Good lighting** from window or lamp
5. **Quiet environment** - minimize background noise
6. **External mic** (optional but recommended - e.g., lapel mic)

### **If using laptop/webcam:**
1. **External webcam** preferred (better quality)
2. **Good lighting** - face lamp or window light
3. **Clean background** - plain wall or blurred background
4. **Test audio** - built-in mic usually fine in quiet room
5. **Screen recording software** if doing demo (OBS, Loom, Zoom)
